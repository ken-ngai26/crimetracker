Q. What does this application do?

A. VPD CrimeTracker is an application for reporting community disorders. It will provide different kinds of disorder reports in categories such as graffiti, suspected drug houses, and suspected abandoned and stolen vehicles.

top

Q. Who will use this application?

A. This application can be used by the public, and by patrol volunteers working with community policing organizations.

top

Q. Do I need to register an account for using the application?

A. Users will be prompted to register an account during their first use of this application. After that, users will be able to access all the features of this application.

Q. What information can I put on the report?

A. Users will be able to attach photos, write a textual description, and provide the location of the disorderly behavior via GPS or manual input.

top

Q. Can this application function without Internet?

A. Users will be able to compose reports and save them directly to the iPhone for future sending purposes. These draft reports are available in the “History” page.

Q. What is the difference between 911 and 311?

A. 911 is to be used only for emergencies (police, fire or medical) where an immediate response is required – when there is an emergency and lives are in danger, immediate action is required or there is a crime in progress.

The Vancouver Police does not have the exclusive responsibility of managing all issues that affect the safety, public health, and welfare of Vancouver's citizens. They do however, recognize the need to form partnerships within the community to identify and resolve such issues.

Problems involving graffiti, garbage, unsafe street conditions and municipal code violations should be reported to the local department holding responsibility. This application and City of Vancouver 311 Contact Centre can help you report these types of disorder.

Q. What types of disorder can I report using this application?

A. Residents and businesses of Vancouver can use this application or call 311 from landlines and cell phones within city limits for information and services, including:

• service needs such as traffic signal repairs

• information requests regarding traffic / parking issues

• concerns regarding parking tickets, noise, graffiti, etc.
